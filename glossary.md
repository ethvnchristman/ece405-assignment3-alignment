# Glossary of Terms: CS336 Assignment 5 (Alignment)

This glossary contains key terms and definitions from the Alignment and Reasoning RL handout, arranged chronologically as they appear in the document.

## 1. Assignment Overview & Motivation
*   **Alignment**: The process of adjusting a language model's behavior to follow specific instructions, human preferences, or reasoning patterns.
*   **MATH Dataset**: A dataset of 12,500 challenging high-school competition mathematics problems, used to evaluate mathematical reasoning.
*   **Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Reasoning**: The process of generating intermediate reasoning steps ("thinking") before arriving at a final answer.
*   **Expert Iteration (EI) / Self-Taught Reasoner (STaR)**: A bootstrapping loop where a model's own correct outputs (verified by a reward function) are used for supervised finetuning.
*   **Verified Rewards**: Rewards based on objective criteria like string matches or unit tests rather than subjective human preferences or model-based judges.

## 2. Methodology & Architecture
*   **Zero-Shot Prompting**: Evaluating a model's performance on a task without any prior finetuning or few-shot examples.
*   **Supervised Finetuning (SFT)**: Updating model weights using a dataset of (prompt, completion) pairs, often used as a "warm-start" for RL.
*   **Reasoning Traces**: Step-by-step solutions (like those from DeepSeek R1) used to teach models how to "think" before answering.
*   **r1_zero Prompt**: A specific template that instructs a model to enclose its reasoning within `<think>` tags and its final answer within `<answer>` tags.
*   **vLLM**: A high-throughput inference engine featuring PagedAttention, used for efficient batched generation during RL.
*   **Answer Parsing**: The process of extracting a symbolic answer from a model's text response to compare against a ground truth.

## 3. RL Training Primitives
*   **Gradient Accumulation**: Accumulating gradients over multiple microbatches to simulate a larger batch size on hardware with limited memory.
*   **Response Mask**: A boolean mask used to ensure the loss is only computed on the model's generated response tokens, not the prompt.
*   **Log-Probabilities (log_probs)**: The natural logarithm of the probability of a specific token given the prefix.
*   **Per-token Entropy**: A measure of the model's uncertainty in its next-token prediction; high entropy means high uncertainty.

## 4. Reinforcement Learning Concepts
*   **Policy ($\pi_\theta$)**: The language model itself, viewed as a stochastic policy that maps states (text prefixes) to actions (next tokens).
*   **Trajectory ($\tau$) / Episode / Rollout**: A sequence of states and actions (tokens) generated by the model for a single prompt.
*   **Return (R)**: The aggregate reward received over a trajectory.
*   **Policy Gradient**: A class of RL algorithms that update policy parameters by performing gradient ascent on the expected return.
*   **REINFORCE**: A vanilla policy gradient algorithm that scales the gradient of the log-probability of actions by the received return.
*   **Baseline (b)**: A value subtracted from the return (e.g., the expected return) to reduce the variance of the policy gradient estimate without introducing bias.

## 5. Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)
*   **Group-Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)**: An RL algorithm that uses the mean reward of a group of outputs for the same question as a baseline, avoiding the need for a separate value function.
*   **Advantage Estimation ($A^{(i)}$)**: A measure of how much better a specific rollout is compared to others in its group.
*   **Importance Sampling**: A technique for estimating properties of a distribution using samples from a different ("old") distribution, using the ratio $\pi_\theta/\pi_{\theta_{old}}$.
*   **GRPO-Clip**: A clipping mechanism (similar to PPO) that prevents the policy from straying too far from the old policy during optimization.
*   **Off-Policy RL**: Training a policy using rollouts generated by a previous version of that policy, allowing for multiple gradient steps per rollout batch.
*   **Length Normalization**: The choice between averaging loss over the sequence length (`masked_mean`) or summing and dividing by a constant (`masked_normalize`).
*   **KL Divergence**: A regularization term used to keep the RL policy close to the initial SFT or base model, preventing "reward hacking" (though often omitted in pure reasoning RL).

## 6. Benchmarks
*   **PALOMA Benchmark**: A benchmark suite for evaluating language model fit across various domains (e.g., C4 100 domains).
*   **Countdown / GSM8K**: Simpler reasoning tasks used as proxies or initial tests for reasoning RL.
